It is hard for me to recall a specific single problem during this sprint, likely because the entire journey was a large complicated problem.
A large part of this sprint was a Javascript Kata, this involves working through challenges over and over again to cement basic ideas.
I hate to admit that I think those basic ideas are far from cemented and are instead floating around the ether of my subconscious.

The real trouble I've had is quite literally a language barrier.
Sometimes you can see the answer but you do not know how to articulate it
It is a bit like when you travel and suddenly something simple like ordering lunch becomes an exam you must study for a stress over

In order to solve these 'foreign language' issues I employed as many problem solving methods as possbile.
I began by outlining the basic funciton of what I need to do, this is called Pseudocode. Rather than tackle the task as a whole, I broke it down into manageable setions
Once I had my road map, I started googling the problem to find the correct syntax and trying anything which looked at least almost correct.
I paid close attention to my error messages and used them to ID the specific line or figure at fault and work from there.
When really stuck it helps to explain the issue outloud, this is called the 'Rubber Ducky method' - I dont have a duck but I annoyed the hell out of my cats. I even spent some time talking to one of my cars
When really stuck, I reached out for help within my co-hort, we have a great bunch of people here and there is always someone willing to lend a hand.
The final problem solving technique I employed is more related to mental healthiness. I'm unsure if it has an offical name but I call it the easy win strategy.
Perhaps you've noted that 'wins' or successes often come in a cluster? You're able to jump from one victory to the next which puts you in a great mental state and helps you proceed.
Easy win strategy is employed when you're in a rut and feel you cant move. Its time to put down the hard task and knock over some easy ones, respond to some emails or do the dishes.
I mowed my lawns and learnt to ride a motorcycle during this sprint, easy wins to build confidence for those hard battles.
